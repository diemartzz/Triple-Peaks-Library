Our staff picks

That our readers love

  • Harry Potter

    A seven-book series, following the journey of a young wizard named Harry Potter. Orphaned as a baby when his parents were killed by the dark wizard Lord Voldemort, Harry grows up with his cruel aunt and uncle before discovering his identity as a wizard on his 11th birthday. The books chronicle the lives of Harry, and his friends, Hermione and Ron, while studying at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

    J.K. Rowling ● Published: 1997-2007

  • The Fault in Our Stars

    A touching story about two teenagers, Hazel Grace Lancaster, a 16-year-old cancer patient, and Gus Waters, who meet at a support group and fall in love. They share a sharp wit and a love of books, especially Grace's touchstone, "An Imperial Affliction" by Peter Van Houten. When Gus gets the chance to meet the book's reclusive author, they embark on an unforgettable adventure.

    John Green ● Published: 2012

  • Me Before You

    Young and quirky Louisa "Lou" Clark moves from one job to the next to help her family make ends meet. She takes a job as a caregiver for Will Traynor, a wealthy young banker paralyzed from an accident. Her cheerful attitude is put to the test and she shows Will that life is worth living. As their bond deepens, their lives and hearts change in ways neither one could have imagined.

    Jojo Moyes ● Published: 2012

  • The Lord of the Rings

    A young hobbit named Frodo Baggins inherits a powerful ring that must be destroyed to prevent the dark lord Sauron from conquering Middle-earth. With a diverse group of companions, Frodo embarks on a perilous journey to Mount Doom. They face many dangers, testing their bravery and bonds as they strive to save their world from darkness.

    J.R.R. Tolkien ● Published: 1954-1955

Upcoming events:

In honor of Joan Feynman: Women in Astrophysics

Mon, January 12

Triple Peaks Library Conference Hall & Online

At this event, which celebrates the library's namesake, our guest lecturer will take us on a journey exploring some of the most prominent female researchers in the world of space science. It's easy to buy into the misconception that this field was mostly dominated by men, but our lecture aims to change that notion and prove that this hasn't been the case since at least the 18th century.
